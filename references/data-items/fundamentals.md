# Fundamentals (`TR.*`)

Headline, **LSEG-calculated** fundamental aggregates — revenue, profit at each margin
level, the balance-sheet totals, and the three cash-flow section subtotals. These are the
convenient, one-code-per-concept fields you reach for when you want a company's top-line
numbers without navigating the full standardized statement. Every code below was **validated
live against the MCP field catalogue on 2026-07-02** (each resolves and returns a value for
`AAPL.O`).

> **`TR.*` vs `TR.F.*` — the crucial caveat.** These are *non-standardized*, LSEG-calculated
> fields. They are a **different family** from the standardized statement line items
> (`TR.F.*`) documented in [balance-sheet.md](balance-sheet.md),
> [income-statement.md](income-statement.md) and [cash-flow.md](cash-flow.md). A `TR.*`
> headline and a `TR.F.*` line item for the *same* concept (e.g. "free cash flow", "EBITDA")
> can return **different values**, because they use different methodologies. Use the `TR.*`
> aggregate for a quick, cross-industry headline; drop to the `TR.F.*` line item when the
> exact accounting definition matters. See [../usage/field-discovery.md](../usage/field-discovery.md)
> for a worked side-by-side.

## How to query

Use `ld.get_data()` with the `Period` parameter — `FY0` for the latest completed fiscal
year, `FQ0` for the latest quarter, ranges like `FY-4:FY0` for a multi-year panel, or `LTM`
for a trailing-twelve-month aggregate. Always add `.fperiod` in multi-period pulls so you can
tell which period each row is, and pass `Scale="6"` (millions) / `"9"` (billions) plus
`Curn` to normalise units and currency. Full mechanics in
[../usage/querying.md](../usage/querying.md).

| Field Code | Title | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `TR.Revenue` | Revenue | Money | Total revenue from business activities for the period (LSEG-calculated headline top line). |
| `TR.TotalRevenue` | Total Revenue | Money | Revenue including all operating revenue streams; a sibling top-line aggregate to `TR.Revenue`. |
| `TR.GrossProfit` | Gross Profit | Money | Revenue less cost of goods sold. |
| `TR.OperatingIncome` | Operating Income | Money | Profit from core operations, after operating costs but before interest and tax. |
| `TR.EBIT` | EBIT | Money | Earnings before interest and taxes. |
| `TR.EBITDA` | EBITDA | Money | Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization. |
| `TR.NetIncome` | Net Income Incl Extra Before Distributions | Money | Bottom-line net income, including extraordinary items, before distributions. |
| `TR.TotalAssets` | Total Assets | Money | Sum of all current and non-current assets on the balance sheet. |
| `TR.TotalDebt` | Total Debt | Money | Total short- and long-term interest-bearing debt. |
| `TR.NetDebt` | Net Debt Incl. Pref. Stock & Min. Interest | Money | Total debt (incl. preferred stock and minority interest) net of cash and equivalents. |
| `TR.TotalEquity` | Total Equity | Money | Total shareholders' equity (common plus preferred plus minority interest). |
| `TR.FreeCashFlow` | Free Cash Flow | Money | LSEG-calculated free cash flow (operating cash flow less capital expenditure). |
| `TR.CashFromOperatingAct` | Cash from Operating Activities, Cumulative | Money | Net cash generated by operating activities (cash-flow statement subtotal). |
| `TR.CashFromInvestingAct` | Cash from Investing Activities, Cumulative | Money | Net cash from investing activities (capex, acquisitions, disposals). |
| `TR.CashFromFinancingAct` | Cash from Financing Activities, Cumulative | Money | Net cash from financing activities (debt, equity, dividends). |

## Access patterns

**1. Headline snapshot** — one company, latest fiscal year, scaled to millions:

```python
import lseg.data as ld
ld.open_session()
df = ld.get_data(
    universe=["AAPL.O"],
    fields=["TR.Revenue", "TR.EBITDA", "TR.NetIncome",
            "TR.TotalAssets", "TR.TotalDebt", "TR.FreeCashFlow"],
    parameters={"Period": "FY0", "Scale": "6", "Curn": "USD"},
)
print(df)
ld.close_session()
```

**2. Multi-year panel** — five years of the profit ladder, one row per fiscal year:

```python
df = ld.get_data(
    universe=["AAPL.O"],
    fields=["TR.Revenue.fperiod", "TR.Revenue",
            "TR.GrossProfit", "TR.OperatingIncome", "TR.EBIT", "TR.EBITDA", "TR.NetIncome"],
    parameters={"Period": "FY-4:FY0", "Scale": "6", "Curn": "USD"},
)
```

## Notes / gotchas

- **`TR.FreeCashFlow` is one of several FCF definitions.** If the exact methodology matters,
  compare against the standardized codes in [cash-flow.md](cash-flow.md)
  (`TR.F.LeveredFOCF`, `TR.F.FOCF`, `TR.F.FreeCashFlowToEq`) — they can differ from the
  `TR.*` headline for the same company and period.
- **No standalone debt/equity or coverage ratio.** For a debt-to-equity ratio, compute
  `TR.TotalDebt / TR.TotalEquity`; there is no ready-made `TR.*` field for it. Several
  *standardized* leverage ratios (e.g. `TR.F.NetDebtToEBITDA`) do exist in the `TR.F.*`
  family if you prefer a pre-computed value.
- **Case-insensitive.** Field codes resolve regardless of case (`TR.Revenue` == `TR.REVENUE`),
  and some concepts have multiple valid names (`TR.CompanyMarketCap` = `TR.CompanyMarketCapitalization`).
- **Silent drops.** An invalid or unentitled field is omitted from the result rather than
  raising — always inspect the returned columns.
- **Fiscal years are company-specific.** `FY0` maps to different calendar dates per company
  (AAPL ends in September); use `CY` periods for calendar-aligned cross-company comparisons.
